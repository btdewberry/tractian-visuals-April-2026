The Zero-Click Paradox: Ranking Higher Does Not Mean Getting Clicks

Average search position vs. average click-through rate for non-branded queries grouped by funnel stage, using the most recent 28-day window. For each stage, the left bar shows CTR (taller = higher CTR) and the right bar shows position quality (taller = closer to position 1).

Key insight: TOFU definitional queries rank closest to position 1 on average (5.5) but produce the lowest click-through rate (6.6%). MOFU queries rank slightly deeper (6.6) but convert visibility into clicks at 10.6%, which is 1.6 times the TOFU rate. This inversion is the clearest signal that Google is answering broad definitional queries directly in the SERP through AI Overviews and featured snippets, while more specific educational and evaluative queries still drive users to the site. BOFU queries rank deepest on average (7.0) but achieve a CTR of 7.9%, well above TOFU despite the weaker ranking. This is why the Q2 plan prioritizes MOFU and BOFU content production: ranking alone does not produce traffic when Google answers the question before the user ever clicks.
