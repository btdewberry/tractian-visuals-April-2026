Search Intent Patterns: Query Volume vs. Click-Through Efficiency

Each bar represents an intent pattern detected across the 897 non-branded queries in the most recent 28-day window. Bars show query count (how many queries match each pattern). Red diamonds show average CTR for that pattern. The three highest-CTR patterns are highlighted in blue.

Key insight: Template and calculator queries have the highest CTR of any intent pattern (16.3% and 6.9% respectively), while broad definitional queries ("what is," "meaning") are the most numerous but produce lower click-through rates. Comparison queries ("vs") combine healthy volume with strong CTR (12.9%), confirming that evaluation-stage content is both in demand and effective at earning clicks. This pattern directly informs the Q2 content mix: lean toward templates, comparisons, and how-to formats rather than pure definitional explainers.

Based on 897 non-branded queries from the last 28 days ending March 31, 2026. Each query is assigned to the first matching pattern only. Queries not matching any pattern are excluded from this view.
