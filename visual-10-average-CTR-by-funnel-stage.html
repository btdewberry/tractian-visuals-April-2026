Click-Through Rate by Funnel Stage: How Content Type Affects Search Performance

Average CTR of existing blog pages classified by funnel stage, using 12-month GSC data. Pages with fewer than 100 impressions are excluded. Red diamonds show average clicks per page at each stage.

Key insight: MOFU content (guides, templates, how-to articles, KPI frameworks) converts search visibility into clicks at 2.6 times the rate of TOFU definitional content. TOFU pages generate 3.2 million impressions across just 32 pages but achieve only 0.14% CTR, confirming that broad "what is" content is increasingly displaced by AI Overviews and zero-click answers. BOFU comparison and evaluation pages sit between the two at 0.19% CTR, reflecting the fact that many of these pages are newer and still establishing ranking authority. However, MOFU pages lead on both CTR and average clicks per page (231 vs. 150 for TOFU and 68 for BOFU), confirming that education and criteria-setting content is currently the strongest conversion engine in the blog. This performance pattern, combined with the BOFU content gap identified in the queries analysis, is why the Q2 plan invests heavily in both mid-funnel depth and bottom-of-funnel gap filling.
